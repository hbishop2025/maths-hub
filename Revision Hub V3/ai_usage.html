Guidance

What is AI?

AI can explain steps, generate examples, spot mistakes, and create practice. It is brilliant for revision when you use it to learn. Do not use it to short-cut the learning.

policy

School policy

Use AI to understand methods and to practise. Do not submit AI-generated answers as your own work.

Golden rule: If it feels like cheating, it is.

check_circle Do’s

  • Ask for worked examples, then try similar questions yourself.
  • Request step-by-step explanations and spot where you went wrong.
  • Generate practice questions at the right difficulty.
  • Ask for simple explanations of keywords.
  • Compare your method to AI and write your own solution.

cancel Don’ts

  • Do not copy answers. You will not learn it and it breaks school rules.
  • Do not enter personal data. Keep prompts anonymous and safe.
  • Do not assume it is always right. Check with notes or a teacher.
  • Do not rely on AI to do homework. Use it to understand, not to submit.
  • Do not ask for exam answers or restricted content.

Common questions

Can I use AI for homework?

Hover or tap to see the guidance.

Yes, to understand methods, check your approach, and create practice questions. No, to generate final answers to hand in.

Is AI always correct?

Hover or tap to see the guidance.

No. Treat AI like a helpful peer. Verify answers with your notes, teacher, or textbook.

What should I write in a prompt?

Hover or tap to see the guidance.

Be specific: topic, year group, what you are stuck on, and the format you want. Steps, hints, or a quiz.

Is using AI cheating?

Hover or tap to see the guidance.

Using AI to learn is fine. Submitting AI work as your own is not. Always produce your own final answers.

Is my data safe?

Hover or tap to see the guidance.

Keep prompts anonymous. Do not share names, emails, or personal info. Follow the school GDPR guidance.

How do I learn with AI?

Hover or tap to see the guidance.

Ask for steps. Try a similar question. Compare. Correct. Repeat. Practice is what sticks.

Prompt ideas (copy and tweak)

Worked example and practice

Explain this Year 9 topic: standard form. Show one worked example. Then give me three practice questions without answers. After I try them, reveal the answers step by step.

Find the error

I solved this indices question but I am not sure it is right: 3^2 × 3^4 = 3^6 → 9 × 81 = 729. Check my working. Point out any errors. Explain the correct reasoning.

Targeted practice with tiers

Give me five questions on factorising quadratics for Year 9. Make two easy, two medium, and one challenging. After each question, wait for my attempt before giving the solution.

Explain like I am new

Explain Pythagoras’ theorem to a Year 8 student using a diagram description and a real-life example. Include two short checks to test understanding.

Make a revision plan

I am in Year 8 and I have a fractions test in two weeks. Make a ten-day revision plan with 20 minute sessions. Include a mix of explanations, practice, and quick checks.

Socratic hints only

I will paste a question. Do not give the answer. Ask me short guiding questions and give one hint at a time. If I am stuck, show the next step only.

Tip: be specific about your year group, topic, and the help you want. Examples, hints, or practice.

This guidance helps you use AI to learn, not to short-cut learning.
