Using AI Sensibly

What is AI?

AI (Artificial Intelligence) is software that can understand prompts and generate helpful responses—like step-by-step explanations, examples, summaries, or quizzes. It’s brilliant for revision if you use it to learn, not to shortcut the learning.

check_circle Do’s

  • Ask for worked examples and then try similar questions yourself.
  • Request step-by-step explanations and spot where you went wrong.
  • Use AI to generate practice questions at the right difficulty.
  • Ask it to explain keywords in simple terms.
  • Compare your method to AI’s and write your own solution.

cancel Don’ts

  • Don’t copy answers—you won’t learn it and it’s against school rules.
  • Don’t enter personal data; keep prompts anonymous and safe.
  • Don’t assume it’s always right—check your answers with your notes.
  • Don’t rely on AI to do homework—use it to understand, not to submit.
  • Don’t ask for exam answers or restricted content.

Common Questions

Can I use AI for homework?

Hover/tap to see the guidance.

Yes—to understand methods, check your approach, and create practice questions. No—to generate final answers to hand in.

Is AI always correct?

Hover/tap to see the guidance.

No. Treat AI like a helpful peer—verify answers with your notes, teacher, or textbook.

What should I write in a prompt?

Hover/tap to see the guidance.

Be specific: topic, level (Year 7–9), what you’re stuck on, and what format you want (steps, hints, quiz).

Is using AI cheating?

Hover/tap to see the guidance.

Using AI to learn is fine. Submitting AI’s work as your own is not. Always produce your own final answers.

Is my data safe?

Hover/tap to see the guidance.

Keep prompts anonymous. Don’t share names, emails, or personal info. Follow school GDPR guidance.

How do I learn with AI?

Hover/tap to see the guidance.

Ask for steps → try a similar question → compare → correct → repeat. Practice is what sticks.

Prompt Ideas (copy & tweak)

Worked Example + Practice

Explain this Year 9 topic: standard form. Show one worked example. Then give me 3 practice questions (no answers). After I try them, reveal the answers step by step.

Find the Error

I solved this indices question but I’m not sure it’s right: 3^2 × 3^4 = 3^6 → 9 × 81 = 729. Check my working, point out errors if any, and explain the correct reasoning.

Targeted Practice (Tiered)

Give me 5 questions on factorising quadratics for Year 9: 2 easy, 2 medium, 1 challenging. After each question, wait for my attempt before giving the solution.

Explain Like I’m New

Explain Pythagoras’ theorem to a Year 8 student using a diagram description and a real-life example. Include 2 quick checks to test understanding.

Tip: be specific about your year group, topic, and the help you want (examples, hints, or practice).
