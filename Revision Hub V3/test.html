What “good AI use” looks like

In maths, AI should help you learn the method, not just copy answers. Use it to break down steps, generate practice, spot mistakes, and quiz you — then verify with your notes and teacher examples.

Best for
Explaining steps

Ask for hints first, then full solutions once you’ve tried.

Best for
Practice & quizzes

Generate questions at your level and mark your working.

Best for
Checking clarity

Ask it to improve explanations, not invent new “facts.”

Do’s & Don’ts

Think of AI like a helpful study partner: it can explain, quiz, and check — but it can’t replace your own working.

task_alt

Do

  • check Ask for hints first, then the full method.
  • check Paste your working and ask: “Where did I go wrong?”
  • check Request practice questions at your year/exam level.
  • check Ask it to mark using a checklist or mark-scheme style.
  • check Verify answers with notes, examples, or another method.
block

Don’t

  • close Copy answers without understanding the steps.
  • close Use AI to complete assessed work unless told it’s allowed.
  • close Trust it blindly on formulas, theorems, or “facts.”
  • close Ask vague questions like “help with algebra” — be specific.
  • close Share personal data (full name, phone, logins, etc.).
Gold standard prompt
“I’m in Year 9. I tried this question and my working is below. Please identify the first mistake, explain why, then give me a hint to fix it. Don’t give the final answer until I ask.”

Hallucinations: when AI sounds confident but is wrong

AI tools can “hallucinate” — that means they may invent steps, definitions, or results that look believable. In maths, always check: definitions, algebra steps, units, and final answers.

Check 1
Verify with another method

Substitute back in, estimate, or solve a simpler version to sanity-check.

Check 2
Compare to your notes

If it uses a method you haven’t learned, ask for your class method instead.

Check 3
Ask it to show each step

Then inspect step-by-step like you would mark someone else’s work.

Useful question to ask AI
“Before you answer, list any assumptions you’re making. If there are multiple methods, show the one we use at school. If you’re unsure, say so.”
