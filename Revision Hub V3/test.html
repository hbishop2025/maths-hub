What good AI use looks like

In maths, AI should help you understand the method. Use it for hints, practice, checking steps, and quizzing. You should still be able to explain the reasoning yourself.

Do's & Don'ts

AI is a study partner — not a shortcut. Use it to learn and verify.

task_alt

Do

  • check Ask for hints first, then a full solution if needed.
  • check Paste your working and ask for the first mistake.
  • check Generate practice at your year/exam level.
  • check Request a mark-scheme style checklist.
  • check Verify with notes, examples, or another method.
block

Don't

  • close Copy answers without understanding the method.
  • close Use AI for assessed work unless your teacher allows it.
  • close Trust it blindly on formulas, theorems, or "facts".
  • close Ask vague questions like "help with algebra". Be specific.
  • close Share personal data (logins, phone numbers, etc.).

star Gold Standard Prompt

"I'm in Year 9. I tried this question and my working is below. Please identify the first mistake, explain why, then give me one hint to fix it. Don't give the final answer until I ask."

Hallucinations

AI can invent steps, definitions, or results that look believable. In maths, verification is part of learning. Confident answers can still be wrong.

1

Use another method

Substitute back in, estimate, or solve a simpler version to sanity-check.

2

Compare with your notes

If it uses a method you haven't learned, ask for the class method.

3

Inspect each step

Treat it like marking: one incorrect step breaks the whole solution.
